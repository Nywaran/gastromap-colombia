Business Understanding

Problem context, objectives, feasibility, and expected impact

Problem Context

Colombia is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, with five distinct natural regions (Andean, Caribbean, Pacific, Orinoquia, and Amazon), each offering unique culinary traditions shaped by geography, climate, and cultural heritage. Gastronomic tourism has become a growing segment worldwide, yet Colombia lacks a systematic, data-driven tool to identify which municipalities hold the highest potential for this type of tourism.

Currently, tourism promotion decisions are made based on intuition, historical reputation, or political priorities rather than quantitative analysis. This leads to over-investment in already-established destinations while municipalities with genuine gastronomic potential remain undiscovered and underfunded.

The problem is compounded by the dispersion of relevant data across multiple government agencies (DANE, MinCIT, IDEAM), each with different formats, coding standards, and update frequencies. No integrated system exists that cross-references geographic, climatic, and tourism registry data to produce actionable insights about gastronomic tourism potential.

Business Objectives

01

Identify High-Potential Zones

Determine which Colombian municipalities have the strongest combination of gastronomic offering, tourism infrastructure, and favorable climate for culinary tourism development.

02

Create a Quantitative Index

Develop a reproducible, transparent scoring system (GTPI) that ranks all municipalities on a 0-100 scale, enabling objective comparison and prioritization.

03

Integrate Open Data Sources

Demonstrate how publicly available government datasets (DIVIPOLA, RNT, IDEAM) can be merged and transformed into a unified analytical dataset using ML techniques.

04

Deploy an Accessible Tool

Build and publish an interactive web application that visualizes results on a map, allowing stakeholders to explore, filter, and understand the data without technical knowledge.

Questions the Model Must Answer

❓

Which Colombian municipalities have the highest potential for gastronomic tourism based on available open data?

❓

How does the gastronomic offering (density and variety) distribute across Colombia's five natural regions?

❓

What role does climate comfort play in determining a municipality's attractiveness for food tourism?

❓

Are there municipalities with strong gastronomy but weak tourism infrastructure (or vice versa) representing development opportunities?

❓

How many municipalities have zero registered gastronomy despite having tourism activity, and where are they?

Project Feasibility

Data Availability ✓

All three datasets (DIVIPOLA, RNT, climate) are publicly available through Colombian government open data portals at no cost. The RNT alone contains 686,967 records — far exceeding the minimum 1,000 required for meaningful analysis.

Technical Feasibility ✓

The required tools (Python, Pandas, Flask, Leaflet.js) are mature, well-documented, and free. The dataset fits in memory on any modern computer. No GPU or specialized hardware needed.

Join Key Availability ✓

All datasets share the 5-digit DIVIPOLA municipality code, enabling reliable cross-referencing without fuzzy matching or manual mapping.

Deployment Feasibility ✓

The processed dataset (444KB JSON) is small enough for free-tier hosting on Render.com. No database server required — the app loads everything into memory on startup.

Risks and Limitations

Climate Model Approximation

Temperature and precipitation are estimated from coordinates and region classification, not from actual weather station measurements. Accuracy is limited for Andean municipalities where altitude varies dramatically within the same coordinates.

High Impact

Static Weight Assignment

The GTPI component weights (35/20/20/15/10) are domain-informed but not data-driven. Different weight configurations could produce different rankings, particularly for mid-tier municipalities.

Medium Impact

Supply-Side Only

The model measures supply (establishments, infrastructure) but not demand (visitor flows, social media sentiment, Google Trends). A municipality may score high on supply but have low actual tourist demand.

Medium Impact

Temporal Aggregation

RNT records spanning 2019-2026 are aggregated without temporal weighting. Recent growth trends are not captured differently from historical presence.

Low Impact

Expected Impact

🏛️

Policy Makers

Evidence-based tool for allocating tourism development funds to municipalities with genuine potential rather than political connections.

📊

Researchers

Reproducible methodology and open dataset for further studies on gastronomic tourism, regional development, or feature weight optimization.

🍽️

Tourism Industry

Entrepreneurs can identify underserved municipalities with high GTPI scores as opportunities for new restaurant, hotel, or tour operator investments.

🎓

Academic Community

Demonstration of CRISP-ML methodology applied to a real-world problem using exclusively open data and open-source tools.
