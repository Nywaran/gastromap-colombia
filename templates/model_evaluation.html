Metric Definitions

MAE

Mean Absolute Error — average absolute difference between predicted and actual GTPI. Lower is better.

MSE

Mean Squared Error — average squared difference. Penalizes large errors more heavily than MAE.

RMSE

Root Mean Squared Error — square root of MSE. In the same units as GTPI (0-100 scale).

R²

Coefficient of Determination — proportion of variance explained. 1.0 is perfect; 0.0 means no better than predicting the mean.

Why Not the Others?

Random Forest

Very close second (F1=0.985 vs 0.990). More stable cross-validation (lower std), making it the safer choice for production if consistency matters more than peak performance. Slightly higher RMSE in regression.

K-Nearest Neighbors

Lowest performance on all metrics (F1=0.969, R²=0.953). More sensitive to the imbalanced class distribution. Also slower at prediction time since it must compute distances to all training samples.

8. Conclusions

All Models Perform Exceptionally

All three classifiers exceed 97% accuracy and 0.99 AUC, confirming that the engineered features contain strong signal for predicting gastronomic tourism potential.

Supervised Validates Unsupervised

The fact that supervised models can learn the GTPI classification with >98% accuracy validates that the unsupervised scoring formula captures real patterns, not noise.

gastro_count Dominates

Feature importance analysis confirms that gastro_count (gastronomy establishment count) is by far the most predictive feature, justifying its 35% weight in the GTPI formula.

Regression Adds Value

Regression models predict the exact GTPI score within ±1 point (RMSE=1.01 for Gradient Boosting), enabling precise scoring of new municipalities without running the full pipeline.
